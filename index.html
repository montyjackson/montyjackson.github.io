My background is in Architectural Engineering (BSc University of Plymouth) and Architectural and Energy Engineering (MRes FIBE2 CDT, University of Cambridge), I am currently working towards a PhD in Hospital Energy Retrofit (University of Cambridge). I have also worked on various projects in a professional capacity, focusing on energy efficiency and decarbonisation in the healthcare, science and technology sector.

My research focuses on improving the energy performance of NHS hospital buildings in England by leveraging open-source datasets and machine learning to inform scalable decarbonisation strategies. I begin with a large-scale quantitative assessment of energy use trends using open data, enriched with contextual and free-text analysis. Building on this foundation, I plan to develop predictive models, data-driven typologies, and a prototype decision-support tool aimed at helping NHS estates teams identify and prioritise decarbonisation interventions.
